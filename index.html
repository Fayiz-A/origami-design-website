Interesting Facts about Camel
  1. Camels' ears are fuzzy
  2. Camels can move in hot and blazing sand because of their special feet
  3. Camels can drink a lot of water at once and remain without drinking water for days

Interesting Facts about Chameleon
  1. The chameleons' eyes can rotate and focus separately on 180-degree arcs, so they can see two different objects at the same time. This gives them a full 360-degree field of vision.
  2. Species of chameleon can be as small as 15 mm (0.59 in) or as large as 69 cm (27 in).
  3. Changing skin color is an important part of communication among chameleons

Interesting Facts about Cicada
  1. Cicadas improve lawns by digging tunnels that allow air into the soil. When they die, their rotting bodies put nitrogen into the soil
  2. Cicadas spend most of their life under the ground
  3. Cicadas are one of the loudest insects on the planet. They produce sound of up to 120 dB (louder than rock concert) that can be heard 0.5 miles away.

Interesting Facts about Pigeon

Interesting Facts about Bear
  1. Some species of Asiatic bear build nests in the trees. They can use these for hiding, eating and even sleeping
  2. Bears are extraordinarily intelligent animals. They have far superior navigation skills to humans; excellent memories; large brain to body ratio; and use tools in various contexts from play to hunting
  3. Bears can walk a short distance on their hind legs alone
